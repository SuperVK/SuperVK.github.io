Generating or inputing keys

 Private Key


Public Key




Keys
You either generate new keys or input a public key gotten from a friend, or your own stored keys. Keep your private key private at all times, and share your public key with your friends so they can encrypt or verify messages. A public key can decrypt a message that is encrypted with a private key, and a private key can decrypt a message that is encrypted with a public key. However, a key can't decrypt the message that it encrypted itself. (EI: a public key can't decrypt a message encrypted by the same public key and the same for private keys)

Assymetric encryption
Give your public key to a friend, and let them encrypt their message with your public key. Then they can send the encrypted message to you, and only you can decrypt the message. (as you are the only person with the private key that can decrypt the message)

Digital Signature
You can sign messages with your private key, and then make your friend verify it. If you given your friend a public keys once, they can always ask you to sign a message with your private key, and then you can send the signed message to them. Then they can verify with your public key and the original messge. An imposter of wouldn't have your private key and wouldn't be able to sign the message.
